# Product Analytics Summary

## Project

Retail Sales Data Warehouse

---

# Objective

The objective of this analysis is to evaluate product performance, identify revenue-driving products, measure sales volume, and support inventory and sales decisions.

---

# Business Questions Answered

1. Which products generate the highest revenue?
2. Which products sell the highest quantity?
3. Which products generate the highest average revenue per sale?
4. Which products appear most frequently in customer orders?
5. What percentage of total revenue does each product contribute?
6. Are there products that have never been sold?

---

# Analyses Performed

## 1. Top Products by Revenue

### Description

Calculated total revenue generated by each product.

### Business Value

Identifies products that drive business revenue.

### SQL Concepts Used

- GROUP BY
- SUM()
- ORDER BY

---

## 2. Best Selling Products by Quantity

### Description

Calculated the total quantity sold for each product.

### Business Value

Measures product popularity.

---

## 3. Average Revenue per Sale

### Description

Calculated average revenue generated each time a product is sold.

### Business Value

Identifies premium products.

---

## 4. Product Sales Frequency

### Description

Measured how many invoices contain each product.

### Business Value

Shows purchasing frequency.

---

## 5. Revenue Contribution

### Description

Calculated the percentage contribution of each product to total business revenue.

### Business Value

Identifies products with the greatest business impact.

### SQL Concepts Used

- Window Functions
- SUM()
- OVER()

---

## 6. Products Never Sold

### Description

Identified products with no associated sales transactions.

### Business Value

Supports data quality investigations and inventory management.

---

# Key Findings

The analysis identifies:

- Revenue-driving products
- High-volume products
- Premium products
- Product popularity
- Revenue contribution
- Unsold inventory

---

# Business Recommendations

- Maintain inventory for top-performing products.
- Increase promotional campaigns for high-revenue products.
- Review pricing strategies for underperforming products.
- Investigate products with no sales.
- Monitor product performance monthly.
